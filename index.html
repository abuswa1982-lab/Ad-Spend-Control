📖 How to read this report (click to expand)

The one number that matters: Net Contribution. If it's green, your ads are making money. If it's red, they're losing money.

How the math works (using one SKU as an example):

You spent $60 on ads → ads brought in $100 of sales (Revenue)
Your margin on this SKU is 25% → you actually keep $25 of that $100 (Expected GP)
Ads cost $60, but profit was only $25 → you LOST $35 on this SKU ($ Wasted)
Absorption = $60 ÷ $25 = 240% → ads ate 2.4× your profit

Anything over 100% absorption means the ads cost more than the profit they made.

The three buckets:

🔴 CUT — Ads ate too much profit (or made none). Pausing these saves you money immediately.

🟡 HOLD — Profitable but tight. Watch them — if they slip, they go to CUT.

🟢 SCALE — Winners. Ads cost only a small slice of profit. Push more budget here.

What to do this period: Look at the top 5–10 items in the CUT list (sorted by $ Wasted). Pause those in Google Ads. The dollar amounts in that column = what you save next period.
